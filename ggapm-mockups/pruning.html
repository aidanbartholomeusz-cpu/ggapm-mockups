Our approach

Species knowledge makes all the difference.

01

Right time, right technique

Pruning at the wrong time or with the wrong cut can set a plant back by a full season. We work to the correct window for each species and use appropriate tools for every job.

02

Structural and health focus

Good pruning isn't just about shape. We remove dead, damaged and crossing growth to improve airflow, reduce disease risk and help plants put energy into the right places.

03

Clean finish, tidy site

All prunings are removed and the site is left tidy. If you'd prefer clippings left for mulching, just let us know.

What's included

Our pruning service covers six key areas.

Most pruning visits combine shrub work with selective tree pruning and a full site clean-up.

01

Shrub and ornamental pruning

Shaping and reducing ornamental shrubs to maintain form, encourage flowering and keep growth in proportion to the space.

Best for: established garden shrubs, roses, feature plants
02

Tree pruning

Crown reduction, deadwooding and shaping for trees up to 8 metres — species-appropriate timing and technique.

Best for: established trees, streetscape trees, overhanging branches
03

Fruit tree pruning

Winter pruning for structure and form, summer pruning for fruit quality — timed correctly for each species.

Best for: citrus, stone fruit, apples, pears, fig
04

Native pruning

Light tip pruning and deadheading for natives — many respond poorly to hard pruning, so species knowledge matters.

Best for: banksias, grevilleas, callistemons, westringias
05

Formative pruning

Early structural pruning of young plants to establish the right framework before problems develop.

Best for: new plantings, young trees, establishing hedges
06

Post-storm clean-up

Removing damaged, broken or hazardous branches after storm events — assessed and cleared promptly.

Best for: after major weather events, trees near structures

Common questions

FAQs.

When is the best time to prune in Sydney?

Most deciduous plants prune best in winter when dormant. Evergreens and natives prefer late autumn or early spring. Hedges trim through the warm seasons. We confirm the right window for each plant.

Do you need a council permit to prune trees?

Some councils require permits for significant pruning of certain tree species. We check council rules for each Eastern Suburbs LGA before quoting major tree work.

Can heavy pruning kill a plant?

Yes, if done badly. Hard pruning at the wrong time or with the wrong cut can stress or kill plants. We use species-appropriate techniques and timing.

How tall a tree can you prune?

We prune trees up to about 8 metres in-house. For larger trees, we work with licensed arborists. We assess height at the site visit and let you know who handles the job.

Do you do tree removal?

We handle smaller removals in-house. For large trees or removals that need a permit, we connect you with licensed arborists we trust.

What about fruit trees specifically?

We prune fruit trees in winter for shape and structure, and in summer for fruit quality. Timing varies by species — citrus, stone fruit, apples and pears each have different windows.
