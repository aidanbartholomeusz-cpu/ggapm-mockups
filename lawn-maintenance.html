Common questions

FAQs.

How often should lawns be mowed in Sydney?

In growing season (September to May), most Sydney lawns need mowing every 1 to 2 weeks. In winter, fortnightly to monthly is usually enough. Buffalo grass grows slower than Kikuyu.

What's the difference between weekly and fortnightly service?

Weekly service keeps the lawn shorter and more uniform, ideal in peak growing season. Fortnightly works well for slower-growing turf and most of the year for Sydney lawns.

Do you bring your own equipment?

Yes. Our team arrives with mowers, edgers, blowers and everything else needed for the visit. You don't need to provide anything.

Can I switch frequency through the year?

Absolutely. Many clients are on weekly through summer and fortnightly through winter. We adjust the schedule with you.

What happens in winter?

Sydney lawns grow slower in winter, so visit frequency drops. We focus on edge work, leaf clearing and occasional mows as needed.

Is there a contract or can I cancel anytime?

No long-term contract. Ongoing lawn care is month-to-month and you can pause or cancel with one week's notice.
